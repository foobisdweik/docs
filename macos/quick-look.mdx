---
title: "Preview files using macOS Quick Look in Spacedrive"
description: "Press Space in Spacedrive to preview images, videos, and documents using macOS Quick Look without opening an external application."
---

Quick Look lets you glance at the contents of a file without opening it in a separate application. In Spacedrive, Quick Look works exactly as it does in Finder: select a file and press the Space bar to see a full-size preview, then dismiss it with another keypress when you're done.

## How to trigger Quick Look

Select any file in Spacedrive — in grid view, list view, or the media viewer — and press **Space**. The Quick Look panel opens immediately. Press **Space** again or press **Escape** to close it.

You can also open Quick Look from the right-click menu by choosing **Quick Look** on any selected file.

## Supported file types

Spacedrive passes files to the macOS Quick Look engine, which handles a wide range of formats natively:

- **Images** — JPEG, PNG, HEIC, WebP, TIFF, GIF, and most other common image formats
- **Video** — H.264, HEVC (H.265), and ProRes (requires macOS codec support)
- **Documents** — PDF, plain text (`.txt`), Markdown, and most common code file types (`.swift`, `.py`, `.js`, `.ts`, `.rs`, etc.)

For file types that macOS doesn't support natively, Spacedrive falls back to displaying the file's name, kind, and size.

## Preview multiple files

You can preview several files one after another without closing and reopening Quick Look:

1. Select multiple files (click the first, then Shift-click or Command-click to add more).
2. Press **Space** to open Quick Look on the selection.
3. Use the **left and right arrow keys** to step through each file in the preview panel.

A counter in the top corner of the panel shows your position within the selection (for example, "3 of 12").

## Quick Look extensions

Spacedrive installs two Quick Look extensions alongside the main app:

**Preview extension** (`SpacedriveQLPreview.appex`) — files that Spacedrive has indexed can be previewed from Finder too, not just from within Spacedrive. If you use Finder to navigate and press Space on a file Spacedrive knows about, Spacedrive's preview extension renders the preview.

**Thumbnail extension** (`SpacedriveQLThumbnail.appex`) — Spacedrive generates thumbnail mosaics for its library folders. In Finder, these folders display a small grid of their contents as the folder icon, giving you a visual summary at a glance.

Both extensions communicate with the Spacedrive daemon through a shared App Group container — no additional permissions or setup are required on your part.

<Tip>
  If thumbnails or previews look out of date after adding new files, try re-indexing the location. In Spacedrive, right-click the location in the sidebar and choose **Re-index location**.
</Tip>

## Closing Quick Look

- Press **Space** — closes Quick Look and returns focus to Spacedrive
- Press **Escape** — also closes Quick Look
- Click anywhere outside the Quick Look panel — closes it on a single-file preview (multi-file previews require a keypress)
