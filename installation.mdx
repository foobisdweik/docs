Spacedrive is a macOS Tahoe-exclusive app. Before you download, confirm that your Mac meets the requirements below, then follow the steps to install and verify that everything is working.

<Warning>
  Spacedrive requires **macOS Tahoe 26.5 or later**. Earlier versions of macOS — including Sonoma and Sequoia — are not supported and there are no backport plans. Check your macOS version in **Apple menu → About This Mac**.
</Warning>

## System requirements

| Requirement | Minimum |
|---|---|
| macOS | Tahoe 26.5 or later |
| Chip | Apple Silicon (M1 or later) |
| Disk space | ~200 MB |

Intel Macs are not supported. Spacedrive is compiled for Apple Silicon only.

## Install Spacedrive

<Steps>
  <Step title="Download the .dmg">
    Go to the [Spacedrive releases page](https://github.com/foobisdweik/spacedrive-native/releases) and download the latest `Spacedrive.dmg`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the disk image">
    Double-click **Spacedrive.dmg** in your Downloads folder. A Finder window opens showing the Spacedrive app icon and an **Applications** folder shortcut.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Drag Spacedrive to Applications">
    Drag **Spacedrive.app** onto the **Applications** folder shortcut in the disk image window. Wait for the copy to finish, then eject the disk image by pressing **⌘E** or clicking the eject button next to it in the Finder sidebar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Launch Spacedrive">
    Open **Applications** and double-click **Spacedrive**. The app opens to a blank window the first time you launch it.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  **Gatekeeper prompt on first launch.** macOS may display an alert that reads *"Spacedrive" is an app downloaded from the internet. Are you sure you want to open it?* Click **Open** to proceed. If you see a different alert saying the app cannot be opened, go to **System Settings → Privacy & Security**, scroll to the bottom of the page, and click **Open Anyway** next to the Spacedrive entry.
</Note>

## Grant Full Disk Access

After the app opens, macOS will prompt you to grant **Full Disk Access**. Spacedrive needs this permission to index folders across your Mac — including your home directory, external drives, and cloud-synced locations. Without it, many folders will be invisible to the indexer.

To grant access:

1. Click **Open System Settings** in the prompt, or go to **System Settings → Privacy & Security → Full Disk Access**.
2. Click the toggle next to **Spacedrive** to enable it.
3. If prompted, enter your Mac password.
4. Return to Spacedrive. The app does not need to be relaunched.

<Note>
  You can grant or revoke Full Disk Access at any time from **System Settings → Privacy & Security → Full Disk Access**. See [macOS permissions](/macos/permissions) for details on all permissions Spacedrive may request.
</Note>

## Verify the installation

Once the app is open and Full Disk Access is granted, you should see a blank Spacedrive window with a sidebar on the left and an empty content area. No libraries or locations are set up yet — that happens in the next step.

If the window does not appear or the app fails to open, see [Troubleshooting common issues](/troubleshooting/common-issues).
